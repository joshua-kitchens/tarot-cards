The Spread Compendium

a guide to arcane card layouts & their mysteries

A tarot spread is a ritual arrangement of cards, each position holding a specific question or lens through which the card's wisdom is interpreted. The same card drawn for "the past" speaks differently than one drawn for "hidden fears." These are the most enduring layouts — from the swift single draw to the grand ten-card inquiry.

The Single Card
1 card · 1–2 minutes

The simplest and most direct reading. One question, one card — the message is undiluted. Ideal as a daily practice to set intention for the day, or when you need a swift, clear answer without the weight of context. Do not underestimate its depth: a single card drawn with focused intention can carry as much insight as a ten-card spread.

The Three Card
3 cards · 5–10 minutes

The most widely used spread in tarot. Three cards read left to right form a narrative arc — the most common being Past · Present · Future, but the positions are flexible. Adapt the labels to the question: Situation · Action · Outcome, Mind · Body · Spirit, or Strength · Challenge · Advice. The power lies in the story the three cards tell together, not in isolation.

The Five Card Cross
5 cards · 10–15 minutes

A cross of four directions with a central card — elegant and versatile. The center card anchors the reading and speaks to the heart of the matter. The four surrounding cards examine it from below (roots), above (potential), behind (past), and ahead (future). This spread is ideal when a question has multiple dimensions that need to be understood in relation to one another.

The Horseshoe
7 cards · 15–25 minutes

Seven cards laid in an arc — the classic horseshoe. This spread offers a panoramic view of a situation: where you came from, where you are, the unseen forces at play, the obstacle, external pressures, advice, and the likely outcome. It is particularly well-suited to situations where you feel stuck or unsure of which forces are shaping your circumstances.

The Celtic Cross
10 cards · 30–45 minutes

The most renowned spread in Western tarot, believed to derive from older divinatory traditions. Ten cards arranged in two parts: a cross of six cards that examines the central situation from every angle, and a staff of four cards revealing the deeper psychological and outcome dimension. The Celtic Cross rewards patience — each card modifies the others, and the full picture only emerges when all ten are read as a conversation rather than ten separate messages.

The Relationship Spread
7 cards · 20–30 minutes

Designed specifically to illuminate a bond between two people — romantic, platonic, professional, or familial. Two columns face each other: one for you, one for them, with a center column revealing what exists between you. This spread is most powerful when approached with genuine curiosity rather than a desired answer — the cards may reveal uncomfortable truths about your own role in the dynamic.

The Year Ahead
13 cards · 45–60 minutes

Thirteen cards — one for each month of the coming year, plus a thirteenth that names the overarching theme of the year as a whole. Traditionally performed at the new year or on a personal anniversary. Each monthly card is not a prediction but an energetic weather forecast: a tone, challenge, or gift the month may carry. Read the months in sequence to spot patterns and turning points across the arc of the year.
